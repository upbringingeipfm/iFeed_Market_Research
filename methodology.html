iFeed Market Research Agency · Methodology

Our Research Methodology

A glimpse at our time-tested and proven information gathering, analysis, formulation, and validation process — authored by a Regulatory Quality Governance Architect with 20 years of cross-domain field experience, structured to match the rigour of Grand View Research while delivering what paid-report firms fundamentally cannot.

Introduction

Throughout each step of our research and reporting process, iFeed institutes industry best practices and international standards of data collection, analysis, and reporting, to provide comprehensive, validated insights to our clients. Our process is rigorous and iterative, and specific to the regulated-life-sciences industries we cover. We continue to validate our work as we uncover new data points and derive new insights, and we work with clients to clearly define research goals and agree upon the methodology we'll apply to each project.

Zero-hallucination discipline governs every output. Every factual claim traces to a citation, a verifiable public source, or an explicit [VERIFY] / [FETCH] / [AUTHOR] flag. Nothing is plausibility-filled. Nothing is fabricated. This is the non-negotiable principle that distinguishes iFeed reports from conventional market research.

Information Procurement

Information procurement is one of the most extensive and important stages in our research process. Quality data is absolutely critical for accurate analysis. We follow a multi-channel data collection process to gather the most reliable and current information possible.

  • Paid databases — access to Grand View Research · MarketsandMarkets · Mordor Intelligence · Fortune Business Insights · PitchBook (deal flow) · EvaluatePharma (pharma pipeline) · IQVIA MIDAS (pharma sales) for cross-source triangulation.
  • Regulatory agency primary sources — FDA (21 CFR, guidance documents, 483 database, warning letters, MAUDE, inspection reports) · EMA · MHRA · CDSCO · PMDA · Health Canada · TGA.
  • Public financial filings — SEC EDGAR (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, DEF 14A) · company annual reports · investor presentations · earnings call transcripts.
  • Industry association data — PhRMA · BIO · ISPE · AAPS · DIA · RAPS · ACRP · ISPOR · ISMPP · MedTech Europe.
  • Academic literature — PubMed · PubMed Central · arXiv · bioRxiv · medRxiv · SSRN.
  • Primary research — structured one-on-one phone interviews with industry experts · questionnaires · field observation from the authored practitioner's network.
  • Grand View / MarketsandMarkets / Mordor report subscriptions — where their publications touch our domains, used for Tier A parity triangulation.
  • Patent databases — USPTO · EPO · WIPO · Google Patents · Espacenet.
  • Clinical trial registries — ClinicalTrials.gov · EudraCT · CTRI · UMIN · WHO ICTRP.

Information Analysis

We mine the data collected to establish baselines for forecasting, identify trends and opportunities, gain insight into buyer demographics and drivers, and so much more. We utilise different methods of data analysis depending on the type of information we're trying to uncover.

Market research approaches

Bottom-up Approach

  • Demand estimation of each product / service across countries or regions summed to form the total market
  • Variable analysis for demand forecast
  • Demand estimation via paid databases, company financials from annual reports
  • Primary interviews for data revalidation and insight collection

Top-down Approach

  • Used extensively for new-product forecasting or analysing penetration level
  • Tool uses invoice-product-flow and penetration models
  • Regression multi-variant analysis for forecasting
  • Extensive use of paid and public databases
  • Primary interviews and vendor-based primary research for variable-impact analysis

Combined / Mixed Approach

Applied for full coverage analysis — derived via commodity flow and demand/consumption models, combining bottom-up and top-down triangulation with regression validation.

Value-Chain-Based Sizing & Forecasting

Supply Side Estimates

  • Company revenue estimation via annual reports, investor presentations, SEC filings, Hoover's
  • Segment revenue determination via variable analysis and penetration modelling
  • Competitive benchmarking to identify market leaders and their collective revenue shares
  • Forecasting via analysing commercialisation rates, pipeline, market initiatives, distribution networks

Demand Side Estimates

  • Identifying parent markets and ancillary markets
  • Segment penetration analysis to obtain pertinent revenue / volume
  • Heuristic forecasting with the help of subject-matter experts
  • Forecasting via variable analysis

Qualitative Functional Deployment (QFD) Modelling is applied for market-share assessment across vendor landscape sections.

Market Formulation & Validation

We finalise market numbers through data normalisation, aligning estimates and forecasts, and refining the report scope and data representation. Our process includes multiple validation levels involving industry experts and quality-control managers, ensuring that only thoroughly validated studies move forward for publication.

Market Formulation

  • Finalisation of market numbers — designed to manage outputs from the Data Analysis step.
  • Data Normalisation — final market estimates and forecasts are aligned and sent to industry experts and in-panel quality-control managers for validation.
  • Finalisation of report scope and data representation pattern.

Multi-level Data Validation

Level 1
In-panel QC team validation
Level 2
Validation by Industry Experts & Key Opinion Leaders
Level 3
Validation by multiple data-analysis models (triangulation)

All three steps run in parallel. The study is forwarded for publishing only if all three levels render validated results. If partial validation only, the study waits for complete validation before moving to Step 2 and then to Publishing.
